Peer-Reviewed Foundation

Built on published science,
not intuition

"Harness design materially shifts task completion on long-horizon coding benchmarks, even with the base model held fixed."

— Lin et al., ICLR 2026

Paniolo is grounded in Agentic Harness Engineering (AHE), published at ICLR 2026 by researchers from Fudan University, Peking University, and Shanghai Qiji Zhifeng. The paper introduces a closed-loop observability framework that autonomously evolves coding-agent harnesses without requiring base-model retraining.

The core insight: harness evolution was bottlenecked by observability, not agent capability. Once the evolution agent receives structured context over a clear action space, it reliably converges on better harness designs. AHE operationalizes this through three matched observability pillars that turn every edit into a falsifiable contract.

AHE outperforms every human-designed baseline (OpenCode, Terminus-2, Codex) and self-evolving baselines (ACE, Training-Free GRPO). The frozen harness transfers to SWE-bench-verified and yields consistent gains across three alternate base-model families — evidence that the evolved components encode general engineering experience, not benchmark-specific tuning.
