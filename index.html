What Paniolo Does

observability

01

Component
Observability

Every editable harness component — system prompt, tools, middleware, long-term memory — gets a file-level representation. The action space becomes explicit and revertible. No more black-box debugging.

Experience
Observability

Peer-Reviewed Foundation

Built on published science,
not intuition

"Harness design materially shifts task completion on long-horizon coding benchmarks, even with the base model held fixed."

— Lin et al., ICLR 2026

Paniolo is grounded in Agentic Harness Engineering (AHE), published at ICLR 2026 by researchers from Fudan University, Peking University, and Shanghai Qiji Zhifeng. The paper introduces a closed-loop observability framework that autonomously evolves coding-agent harnesses without requiring base-model retraining.

The core insight: harness evolution was bottlenecked by observability, not agent capability. Once the evolution agent receives structured context over a clear action space, it reliably converges on better harness designs — turning every edit into a falsifiable contract.

AHE outperforms every human-designed baseline and self-evolving baseline. The frozen harness transfers to SWE-bench-verified and yields consistent gains across three alternate model families — evidence that evolved components encode general engineering experience, not benchmark-specific tuning.
